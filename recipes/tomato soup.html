kapsalon

Description

Dutch tomato soup is a nostalgic favorite, often served as a starter during family dinners or as a hearty lunch. Unlike a plain tomato broth, the Dutch version is known for being slightly thick, vibrantly red, and traditionally packed with tiny, savory meatballs.

It strikes a perfect balance between the acidity of the tomatoes and a hint of sweetness. It is usually served with a dollop of crème fraîche and a side of crusty bread or salted crackers, making it the ultimate soul-warming dish for a rainy day.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg ripe tomatoes (or 2 cans of peeled tomatoes)
  • 1 liter vegetable or beef stock
  • 150g small beef meatballs (pre-cooked or seasoned)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 clove of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • A splash of heavy cream or crème fraîche
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Steps

  1. Sauté the Base: Heat a little oil in a large pot and sauté the onion and garlic until soft and fragrant.
  2. Simmer: Add the tomato paste and cook for one minute, then add the tomatoes and stock. Bring to a boil and let it simmer for about 15 minutes.
  3. Blend: Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until it is completely smooth.
  4. Add Meatballs: Drop the small meatballs into the smooth soup and let them simmer for about 5-10 minutes until they are heated through and cooked.
  5. Season: Taste the soup and add salt, pepper, or a pinch of sugar if the tomatoes are too acidic.
  6. Garnish: Ladle the soup into bowls, add a swirl of cream and a sprinkle of fresh parsley. Serve hot!
